Case study

Bilingual Salary Certificate Automation

A controlled request-to-PDF workflow for salary certificates and NOCs, with templates, approvals and verification.

Last updated: 10 July 2026Hossam Aly

This page documents the design approach without exposing confidential company or employee information.

The operational problem

Salary certificates are repetitive but high-risk documents. Manual copying can introduce errors in names, salary components, addressee wording, language, signatory and date.

Design approach

The employee selects the certificate type, language and destination. The system reads verified employee data, applies destination-specific wording, routes the request for approval and generates the final document from a controlled template.

Business rules

Salary components must follow the organization’s approved definition. Housing or transport provided in kind should not be represented as cash. Template rules, signatories and optional serial numbers are maintained in settings rather than embedded throughout the code.

Verification

A QR code can lead to a public verification page that confirms document number, issue date, employee name and validity without exposing salary details.

Security

Employee access can be limited to the company network or protected with one-time verification. Approval history and document generation events should be logged.

Outcome

The workflow reduces manual retyping, keeps wording consistent and creates a verifiable audit trail from request to issuance.

Next step

A robust implementation begins with the actual policy, data sources, roles and exception scenarios—not with the interface alone.
