Document Expiry Management System

A practical architecture for tracking employee and company documents, assigning ownership and sending controlled reminders.

Last updated: 10 July 2026Hossam Aly
This page documents the design approach without exposing confidential company or employee information.

The operational problem

Document-expiry tracking often starts as a spreadsheet with dates and colors. The real risk is not the absence of a date; it is unclear ownership, inconsistent alert thresholds, duplicate records and no evidence that a reminder was acted upon.

Design approach

The system separates document types, entities, owners, status rules and notification settings. A normalized register holds one record per document instance, while configurable thresholds determine upcoming, urgent and expired states.

Core controls

Role-based access limits who can edit master data. Validation prevents impossible dates and missing owners. Every alert run is logged, and repeated notifications are governed by frequency settings rather than being sent on every page refresh.

Reporting

The dashboard shows documents by company, category, owner and risk status. Exception views focus attention on missing attachments, overdue renewals and records with incomplete responsibility assignments.

Technology

Google Sheets can serve as the controlled data layer for a small or medium internal workflow, with Apps Script handling validation, scheduled alerts, PDFs, email and a lightweight web interface. The architecture can later move to a database without changing the business rules.

Outcome

The value is a clearer control environment: every document has an owner, every status follows the same rules, and every notification can be traced.

Next step

A robust implementation begins with the actual policy, data sources, roles and exception scenarios—not with the interface alone.
